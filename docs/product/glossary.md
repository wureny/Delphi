This glossary defines the key terms used throughout the Delphi project.

Asset

An investable object or theme, such as a company, stock, sector, factor, or macro narrative.
All theses and evidence link to at least one asset or theme.

Thesis

A structured investment belief about an asset or theme.
A thesis includes a title, summary, bull and bear cases, key assumptions, risks, catalysts, time horizon, conviction score, freshness, related evidence and decisions.

Assumption

A proposition that must remain true for the thesis to hold.
Example: "Gross margins will expand due to scale."

Evidence

A source-backed observation that supports, contradicts or updates a thesis or assumption.
Evidence must include the source, date, excerpt or reference, affected asset/thesis/assumption, impact classification (support/contradict/neutral/uncertain), confidence level, reasoning summary and citation.

Counter-evidence

Evidence that weakens, contradicts or falsifies a thesis or assumption.
Counter-evidence is first-class in Delphi and must be highlighted.

Catalyst

An upcoming event that could affect a thesis, such as an earnings release, regulatory decision, product launch, analyst day, or index event.

Risk

A factor that could materially harm a thesis if realised.
Risks may include competition, execution issues, regulatory changes, macro headwinds or technology risks.

Decision

A human investment or research choice, such as opening or closing a position, increasing or decreasing size, changing conviction, or initiating deeper research.
Decisions are recorded with rationale and evidence.

Decision trace

A structured log of how a belief or decision evolved over time.
It captures timestamp, actor, prior thesis state, new evidence, changed assumptions, the decision taken, rationale, sources and follow-up actions.
Decision traces are auditable and are not the model's private chain-of-thought.

Conviction

A qualitative or quantitative measure of confidence in a thesis, expressed on a 0-100 scale and summarised as low, medium, or high.
Conviction may change over time as evidence accrues.

Freshness

A measure of how recently a thesis has been reviewed or updated.
Old or stale theses may warrant a re-examination.

Evidence inbox

The feed where new information enters Delphi.
Items are classified by their relation to assets, theses and assumptions and are either accepted, corrected or dismissed by the user.

What changed?

A core interaction where the user asks Delphi to summarise material changes since the last review.
The system reports new supporting or contradicting evidence, weakened or strengthened assumptions, and prompts the user to consider whether a decision should be recorded.

Ontology / Context graph

Internal infrastructure representing relationships among assets, theses, assumptions, evidence, counter-evidence, catalysts, risks, sources, decisions and time.
It is not exposed to the user and does not claim deterministic causality; it organises research memory for tracing impacts.
