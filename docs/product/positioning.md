Delphi positions itself not as another "AI-powered financial research tool" but as a living thesis workspace for active investors.

Why existing tools fall short

Incumbent platforms like Bloomberg, FactSet and AlphaSense excel at data coverage, document search and financial analytics.
Agentic assistants and RAG-based chatbots can answer questions or summarise documents, but they do not maintain ongoing investment judgement.
None of these tools preserve the reasoning that led to a position, track how new information changes conviction, or surface counter-evidence in a structured way.

Delphi's wedge

Delphi's core wedge is judgement maintenance.
It organises the user's own research - notes, memos, watchlists, models - and ties external information to personal theses.
It keeps a history of why a thesis was held, what evidence supported it, which assumptions would falsify it, and how conviction changed over time.
When new information arrives, Delphi answers "What does this mean for my thesis?" rather than merely summarising it.

Target users

* Small and mid-sized funds - these investors need research infrastructure but lack internal engineering teams like mega-funds.
* Emerging managers and family offices - they manage concentrated portfolios and must justify decisions to LPs but cannot afford large research staff.
* Thematic public equity analysts - they operate in fast-moving, narrative-driven sectors where evidence is scattered across filings, transcripts, expert notes, and industry publications.
* Solo portfolio managers - individuals running concentrated public equity books who need to track their reasoning and updates.

Differentiation summary

* Personal research memory - Delphi connects to the user's existing documents and notes, so it knows the context of their coverage universe.
* Living thesis map - it maintains relationships among assets, assumptions, evidence and decisions, updating them over time.
* Counter-evidence surfacing - contradictory information is highlighted, not suppressed.
* What-changed engine - the product focuses on change detection, telling users when a key assumption is impacted rather than flooding them with news.
* Decision trace ledger - decisions are captured with reasoning and evidence, enabling clear post-mortems and LP updates.
* AI as assistant, not oracle - Delphi uses AI to classify, summarise and detect changes, but leaves judgement and decision-making to the user.
