These statements clarify what Delphi will not do.

No personalised investment advice

Delphi does not provide buy/sell recommendations, price targets or personalised financial advice.
It organises research and highlights changes; investment decisions remain the user's responsibility.

Not a trading platform

Delphi does not execute trades, manage orders or connect to brokerage APIs.
It may document decisions but will not place or cancel orders.

Not a generic financial chatbot

Delphi is not intended for open-ended conversations about macroeconomics, general market gossip or non-research topics.
Chat may be a secondary interaction mode, but the primary interface exposes theses, evidence and changes.

Not a data terminal replacement

Delphi does not aim to replace Bloomberg, FactSet, AlphaSense or other comprehensive market data terminals.
It can integrate with external data sources where licensed but its value lies in organising the user's research, not replicating entire data sets.

No deterministic market predictions

Delphi does not claim to predict prices or guarantee outcomes.
It helps users reason about evidence and assumptions but acknowledges the inherent uncertainty of markets.

No exposure of model chain-of-thought

Delphi does not show the AI model's internal reasoning steps as decision trace.
Decision traces record human rationale and evidence; the model's chain-of-thought remains hidden.
